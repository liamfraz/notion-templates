## Description

**Your construction projects are scattered across spreadsheets, email chains, and filing cabinets. SiteOS fixes that.**

If you're a construction project manager, you already know the problem. Your defect list lives in an Excel spreadsheet that three people have different versions of. Your RFIs are buried in email threads that nobody can search properly. Variations are tracked in a Word document that hasn't been updated since last month. Progress claims are in a folder structure that made sense to whoever set it up in 2019, but nobody else.

You've probably looked at project management software. Procore costs $50,000+ per year. Aconex requires an IT department to implement. Monday.com and Asana don't understand construction — they think "defect" means a software bug and "variation" means an A/B test.

**SiteOS is a Notion template built specifically for construction project management.**

It was designed by someone who has spent 3+ years in the Australian construction industry, managing real projects at a Tier 1 builder. Every database, every property, every view exists because it solved a real problem on a real site.

SiteOS gives you nine connected databases that talk to each other. Log a defect on one of your projects, and it automatically shows up on that project's page. Submit a variation, and the cost rolls up to your project's total variation position. A subcontractor's insurance expires, and you see it flagged before they set foot on site.

**This is not a generic project management template with the word "construction" swapped in.** There are no Gantt charts pretending to replace your construction program. No CRM features you'll never use. No "sprint planning" boards. Just the things construction PMs actually need, structured the way construction actually works.

### How it works

You duplicate the template to your Notion workspace. You import the database CSV files (included) to populate the structure with sample data. You follow the step-by-step Setup Guide to connect the databases with relations. Then you delete the sample data, enter your own projects, and you're running.

The whole setup takes under an hour. No technical knowledge required — if you can use a spreadsheet, you can set this up.

### What makes it different

Most "construction" templates online are built by template designers who spent 20 minutes reading about construction on Wikipedia. They include things like "Milestone Tracker" and "Resource Allocation Matrix" — things that sound professional but don't match how construction PMs actually work.

SiteOS includes the things you actually do every day:
- Write a site diary at the end of shift
- Log defects during site walks
- Track RFI response deadlines so you can chase consultants before they become critical-path delays
- Monitor variation approvals so you know your contract position
- Record safety incidents properly, with root cause analysis and corrective actions
- Track subcontractor compliance — insurance, licensing, inductions
- Manage progress claims and retention

Everything connects back to your projects. Open a project page and see every defect, every RFI, every variation, every safety incident, every claim, and every diary entry — all in one view.

## Who Is This For?

**SiteOS is built for:**

- **Construction Project Managers** running 1-5 active projects who need one place to track everything
- **Site Managers and Superintendents** who need to log daily site diaries, defects, and safety incidents efficiently
- **Construction Company Owners** (small to mid-size builders) who want visibility across all projects without enterprise software costs
- **Contracts Administrators** tracking RFIs, variations, and progress claims
- **Graduate/Cadet Engineers** building their project management toolkit early in their career

**SiteOS works for:**
- Commercial construction (offices, retail, mixed-use)
- Residential multi-storey (apartments, townhouses at scale)
- Institutional (schools, hospitals, government buildings)
- Civil and infrastructure (with minor customisation to database properties)

**SiteOS is probably not for you if:**
- You need full BIM integration or 3D model management
- You require formal AS/ISO document control with transmittals and revision tracking
- You already have Procore, Aconex, or similar and are happy with it
- You manage single residential homes (this is built for commercial-scale complexity)
