## Description

**Your projects are scattered across Jira, Asana, spreadsheets, and Slack threads. PM OS fixes that.**

If you're a project manager, you already know the problem. Your task list lives in Jira but your stakeholder notes are in a Google Doc that hasn't been shared with the right people. Your risk register is an Excel file attached to an email from six weeks ago. Decisions get made in Slack threads that disappear into the void. Your weekly status report is a copy-paste job from four different tools that takes two hours every Friday afternoon.

You've probably tried to consolidate. Monday.com is powerful but overwhelming — you spend more time configuring boards than managing projects. Asana is clean but shallow — the moment you need stakeholder mapping or a decision log, you're back in spreadsheets. Jira is built for engineering teams, not for the PM who needs to see the full picture across workstreams, risks, and dependencies.

**PM OS is a Notion template built specifically for how project managers actually work.**

It was designed by someone who manages real projects — not by a template designer who read a PMP study guide. Every database, every relation, every dashboard exists because it solved a real problem on a real project. The weekly review system exists because status meetings kept catching people off guard. The decision log exists because "I thought we agreed on that" derailed an entire sprint. The risk register exists because surprises kill projects.

PM OS gives you eight connected databases that talk to each other. Create a task under a project, and it rolls up to your project dashboard automatically. Log a risk, and it appears on that project's risk profile. Record a decision, and it's linked to the stakeholders who were in the room and the tasks it affects. Run your weekly review, and every overdue task, escalating risk, and pending decision surfaces without you having to dig.

**This is not a generic productivity template with "project management" in the title.** There are no habit trackers. No meal planners. No "life dashboard" widgets. Just the things PMs actually need — stakeholder management, risk tracking, decision logs, weekly reviews — structured the way project management actually works.

### How it works

You duplicate the template to your Notion workspace. You import the database CSV files (included) to populate the structure with sample data. You follow the step-by-step Setup Guide to connect the databases with relations. Then you delete the sample data, enter your own projects, and you're running.

The whole setup takes under an hour. No technical knowledge required — if you can use a spreadsheet, you can set this up.

### What makes it different

Most "PM" templates online are built by template designers who think project management is a to-do list with due dates. They include things like "Priority Matrix" and "Goal Setting Framework" — things that sound professional but don't match how PMs actually work day-to-day.

PM OS includes the things you actually do every week:
- Run a weekly review to surface what's overdue, at risk, and blocked
- Track stakeholders and know who needs to be informed, consulted, or escalated to
- Maintain a risk register so you're managing risks before they become issues
- Log decisions so nobody asks "wait, didn't we already decide this?" ever again
- Monitor task dependencies across workstreams so nothing falls through the cracks
- Manage meeting actions and follow-ups so decisions actually turn into outcomes
- Report project health at a glance without spending two hours building a status slide

Everything connects back to your projects. Open a project page and see every task, every risk, every decision, every stakeholder, and every meeting note — all in one view.

## Who Is This For?

**PM OS is built for:**

- **Project Managers** running 2-8 active projects who need one place to track everything across workstreams
- **Team Leads** who manage deliverables across multiple contributors and need visibility without micromanaging
- **Program Managers** overseeing a portfolio of related projects and need to see cross-project risks and dependencies
- **Freelance Consultants** managing client projects who need a professional, repeatable system without enterprise tool costs
- **Aspiring PMs** building their project management toolkit and learning proper PM discipline early

**PM OS works for:**

- Technology projects (software, infrastructure, platform migrations)
- Marketing projects (campaigns, launches, brand initiatives)
- Product development (feature delivery, roadmap execution)
- Consulting engagements (client deliverables, workstreams, milestones)
- Operations projects (process improvement, system implementations, org changes)

**PM OS is probably not for you if:**

- You need enterprise PPM with resource leveling, capacity planning, and portfolio optimisation across 50+ projects
- You require Gantt chart dependencies with critical path analysis and baseline tracking
- You already have Monday.com, Asana, or Smartsheet and your team is happy with it
- You manage a PMO with formal stage-gate governance that requires tool-specific compliance reporting
